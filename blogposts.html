Week 8 : The World in the www / Semantic Markup

This week was a very interesting one. I’ve always known that the internet itself is an infinite void of information, but it was very interesting to learn just how many different types of information can lie within, and how that effects our every day lives. By going through the lectures and readings, ive stumbled across three internet geographies. Access and enablement, participation and representation which have been defined according to Graham et all, 2015, Towards a study of information geographies.

In a nutshell, this describes to us “who can, what they can do and who they are. This is the epitome of the www and explains to us just how many people can participate in many different activities, contribute and represent themselves. This ties in perfectly as this week we went deeper into the use of semantic markup, microdata and data attributes.

Within our websites, we are the ones that seek to create a piece of work that represents us and what we can do. The “access and enablement, participation and representation” By learning these data functions it becomes a lot easier for us to achieve this and have our work more easily accessible as through the use of semantic markup, google will be able to better read and represent our work. When people search for similar topics, semantic markup helps push out our topics to those who search for it. The use of the metadata tags also help display to others information about us and our website. Its quite amazing just how everything is linked to one another.

Week 9 : Digital Inequalities / CSS

This week I’ve learnt about digital inequalities as well as expanded my knowledge in CSS. Now I always knew about the negatives online. Almost everyone who has access to the internet has experienced some form of negative experience, or in other words…. Digital inequality.

Upon further research I have stumbled across a website that categorizes digital inequalities into three different types. The universal access divide, The Social divide and the gender divide. (Flynn, 2021) It is known within the universal access divide that not everyone is suited for the internet. There are people out there who simply do not understand how it functions or who are restricted from accessing it.

Such examples can be seen with people with disabilities, who could struggle to navigate online. People in poorer countries who do not have the money to even access these devices. Theres people with low literacy or disorders will struggle to navigate online, read and understand what is being presented, having to resort to asking for help in person which can be tedious and costly. There are so many inconsistencies when it comes to the limitations of the digital world. In this day and age, it is rapidly advancing and countries are pushing their people to upgrade to the latest equipment and go “online” so that they can keep up to date. This puts a toll on many people as they are encouraged to “go with the flow” but simply lack the resources or skills to do so.

Social inequality then comes into play here as the people who have access to these resources vs the people who don’t. When it comes to socializing, it becomes very difficult for someone who has access to the internet to speak to someone who doesn’t. Since the internet stores every piece of information you can imagine, for those who cannot see it, it becomes difficult to socialize with and could potentially create rifts. Majority of the younger generations these days only socialize online as they do not lack the skills to socialize in person! And when they do meet someone in person, unless they are also involved with the internet and its many wonders, they will struggle to make conversation with.

Last of the three mentioned within this article is the gender divide. There are always stereotypes of how woman should be the housekeepers whilst the men should be the ones who go out to work and earn. This can affect a lot of women in the sense that the digital world is stereotyped to be more fitting for men. Men are the ones who should be experiencing these new wonders and finding work on there whilst the woman should stick and do what they always do. These stereotypes also end up carrying themselves into gaming environments and social media, where many sexist people will use these stereotypes against woman, or even against men in the sense that “men are slobs who sit around and game all day on their pc”

This opens up for a lot of negative influences and impacts when it comes to gender. But as always, these are just a few of the many things that come along with these topics, the list of inequalities ae endless. All of these “digital inequalities tend to mirror existing social inequalities in terms of socio-economic status, education, gender, age, geographic location, employment status, and race” (Lutz, 2019) as mentioned above. Our best bet would be to try and prevent them through certain practices, but even so, the internet will always have a bunch that cannot stray away from the negativity. We just need to ignore it and look for the positive ones.

Alongside all of the information I have learnt about digital inequalities this week, I have also further expanded my CSS knowledge with the use of flexboxes and grids. Whilst working on my website for the first assignment, there were SO many unique ways I wanted to structure my website and decorate it, I remember searching for ways to create these structures on YouTube and majority of them mentioned flexbox. Since it was something, I’ve never heard about I decided to go mor basic since I feared it would be difficult to approach, and also since we have not learnt it. This week showed me that not only is flexbox super useful and easy, but in future whilst researching, I should take any opportunity to watch the videos and try understand what is happening as you never know if you will need or come across that in the future. That is the key to becoming a successful dev!

Week 10 : Decolonising Technology / CSS

I’ve learnt a lot this week when it comes to how influential AI really is in this day and age. Firstly, I’ve looked at Verdi’s The road to techno colonialism and examined just how much ai has shaped our world and its economic decisions. Before, power came from resources and military power. Whilst this is still the case ai is slowly taking over that. It has given many other countries significant more power especially those that were already prominent with technology.

“AI has the potential to drive global productivity upwards by automating routine tasks, enhancing jobs that survive and disrupting entire industries.” (Verdi, 2020) Countries can use AI for more economical and military gain that was never possible before. Overthrowing some countries or being overthrown... all depending on the technological resources that country has. Not only this but AI itself is colonialising us! We are in the technological era and by scanning through multiple readings, AI has already begun to take over many of our jobs and responsibilities as humans. While its beneficial from the point because then it will be more consistent and efficient, its negative as we are slowly being replaced by technology itself.

Even though some may find a negative in this, the technological impact isn’t all that bad. It also opens room to “colonise” in different ways, such as to spread digital information throughout the world that not everyone knows about. This can be especially powerful when looking at cultures. “Representations of current ideas may vary, and people with different cultural backgrounds may have novel ideas that differ from those of the north-western cultures” (Tedre 2006, 128) Society has become very westernized, so with technology that opens up room for many cultures and ideologies to be recognized and represented. This will not only help us “rethink how to teach the use and development of technological tools in culturally relevant ways.” (Tedre 2006, 129) But this opens up a huge learning ground for our society and only helps us acknowledge and truly appreciate our diversity.

It just goes to show just how much technology has an impact in this day and age. It’s truly amazing that I am studying something that falls under that, where I could potentially be seen working with technology to create and be apart of something truly marvellous in the future. Week by week I learn more and more just how important this course is. AI and IT is the future, it has already begun influencing our world so much. In the very future the digital colonialism could very well overthrow many jobs that ai can do better than humans, but those technologies will always need a couple programmers and designers working behind them.

References

Verdi, G 2020. The Road to Technocolonialism Found at https://www.internetjustsociety.org/the-road-to-techno-colonialism

Tedre, M. Sutinen, E. Kähkönen, E. Kommers, P. 2006 Ethnocomputing: ICT in cultural and social context
