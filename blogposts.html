Week 1 : Imagining the Internet / HTML Fundamentals.
I have learnt a lot this week, and we barely even started!
In this course we will be not only learning about how the WWW works, but we will be contributing to it. We will be creating out own " personal hypertext system used for keeping track of personal information on a distributed project” (Berners, 792) AKA creating our own website!

I started off by choosing a reading, in more particular, “Tim Berners-Lee et al. – 1994 – The World Wide Web”
I have chosen this one as everything has the WWW at its route, and I wanted to learn more about how its all connected. The first step was starting with GitHub. Fortunately, I have worked with GitHub last year so I did not have to install it for understand how it works as I already did that. Then it clicked to me, after reading the reading just how GitHub perfectly embodies the WWW. “The World-Wide Web (W3) was developed to be a pool of human knowledge, which would allow collaborators in remote sites to share their ideas and all aspects of a common project” (Berners, 792) The second I read this line, GitHub immediately came to mind, and how we will be uploading our thoughts and ideas as a common project onto there, eventually forming our website which will be for everybody to see, and the base of it all starts with HTML, the “common basic language of interchange for hypertext”. (Berners, 795)

Majority of the content within the web consists of HTML so I was very excited to dive deeper and learn it. Upon watching the lecture, I noticed that so far, HTML appeared very easy. It mainly consisted of being the base structure of the website, that includes the menus, lists, contact lists etc, but I realised that HTML was the basics of a much larger picture, it was just the shell of the complicated WWW I am really looking forward to all this as this is what I see myself doing in the future. My goals for this course are to be able to properly understand how the WWW works, and how I could make uploading my one website VS the billions out there stand out from the rest. I also look forward to learning the basics of code, as IT and coding is the future. My goal is to understand the many languages we will learn so I can apply them well in future. I myself am not a very logical person, I am more artistic, and the fact that this course embodies design, with front end coding is exactly why I think this course is perfect for me and I cannot wait to keep learning more.
Week 2 : Internet Histories / HTML Fundamentals.
This was a very exciting week, as I have finally begun experimenting with the fundamentals of HTML.
I was introduced to various different languages and histories within this week, and in particularly we have started focusing on HTML. Upon watching the lecture I’ve decided to practice along and make my own notes. It was very interesting coming across all the different functions HTML has to offer. They looked simple, but immediately I had ideas going through my head on how I could use these to create a bigger picture.

Ive always struggled with high end coding and so far, as ive been practicing HTML its very easy for me to do stuff completely on my own by figuring it out and playing around with it.

I was a little bit confused at first when it came to Semantic markup, as I didn’t really understand what I would use where, but thankfully I stumbled upon Bruce Lawsons article on “Why you should choose HTML5 article Over section”. It gave me a better understanding of how these functions work and exactly what they do. I didn’t know that just a simple function can help the browser categorise your information for easy access upon the WWW, it creates content. I’ve realized just how important semantic markup is as when applied poorly, your website can easily get lost within the billion others on the Internet.

I have also started looking into wireframes this week. I’ve begun searching over the internet for inspiration on the layout and structure of my website. So far, I have found a few that peaked my interest and I have made wireframes for the basis of my website based on what I’ve found. It appears all software developers webs (what I was mainly looking into) have a similar approach. I want to create something similar but with my own artistic twist.

I am excited to get started and see what I can create using these HTML fundamentals. I know I will have to research a lot more as what was mentioned in the lecture is only but a fragment.
Week 3 : Interaction and the WWW / CSS Fundamentals.
I was worried at first to begin with CSS. I thought to myself “I don’t even know HTML yet; how can I start learning a new language already.”. I was confused as why we needed to use multiple languages for a website, couldn’t HTML just do it all? My questions were all answered upon watching this week’s lecture.

While…TECHNICALLY HTML could create styling, it’s much more efficient to make use of CSS instead. I began experimenting and working on some styling techniques and honestly, its even more fun than HTML! I thought I properly understood semantic markup from last week, but upon watching my lecturer use them for styling techniques, I was left confused on exactly why someone would use ID's vs Classes, however, upon practicing the examples along with the video, i quickly started understanding its uses and their differences.

I have followed through the lecture and created the website shown within. I felt so proud making something like this, even though I basically just followed the video. It was still fun none the less and I have learnt a lot from practicing this. I will definelty use some of the techniques I have learnt from this video for future practices.


Ive learnt a lot about how interactivity is key and how i need to create my website to respond in a certain fashion. I need to create events within my webiste that will make it easier for the user to navigate around. Not only that but i need to use these styling techniques ive learnt to create a page that flows nicely, and isnt an eyesore.

Thankfully, having studies art in highschool i have a very good understanding of which colours work well together and which dont. This will definetly aid me in creating a successful webpage. From what ive seen so far, many webpages use blue. I definetly think i will be using some form of blue for mine as it is a gentle colour and works well with many others.

I also, unfortunately couldn’t get through the reading for this week, but i will definetly catch it up on Monday.
Week 4 : Interaction Design for The Web / JavaScript Fundamentals.
This week we started with a new language, JavaScript. So far this was the least enjoyable language as it reminds me a lot of last year and using C# in game design. This is the more logical side of coding and unfortunately, I am not strong in this aspect, purely because I don’t understand JavaScript that well. The exercises we had to do for this week were a pain and I didn’t even complete majority of them as I didn’t know how to approach it. Not only because of that but because I was a little behind with work and only started working on this later in the week.

Besides the complications with Java, I did however officially start with my website this week. I made a basic home screen using the knowledge I have learnt from the past weeks. I have also payed special attention to this week and last weeks readings regarding Interaction as I didn’t want to create a hard to navigate website.

I’ve learnt a lot this week about UI/UX and how to use them to create a successful website. I looked through a lot of the factors mentioned within these readings such as the systems, interactions, symbolic representations etc, and have laid out myself a few goals thanks to this. I was able to come up with a couple questions and approaches such as “who is this targeted towards to?” “What am I trying to achieve?” and I have based my work so far off of that. A lot of my questions were created through reading “The Basic Principles of User Design” as it brought to my attention some questions, I need to be asking myself, as well as approaches I should look at when creating a website, how I can best use UI/UZX to achieve this.

Thanks to all these readings and due to researching other websites, I’ve decided to go with a simplistic approach, not only because “Predictable design means it’s easy to understand how to use functions without instruction.” (UXPin, 2020) but because every website I’ve seen thus far consists of a simplistic design as well as being straight forward towards the user on what it has to offer.

I aim to create an experience that is just like this, a basic portfolio showcasing my skills and what i have to offer, and that is what ive begun working on.

Week 7 : The Ethics of UX / Review
I am currently headed in a good position with my website. I have received a lot of feedback from my peers based off of my initial design and a lot of them suggested ways for improvement. A lot of my structuring felt very cluttered, an “information overload” and so I have decided to change the layout of it all over again.

I had to create new wireframes for the new ideas I had. Luckily, I didn’t have to scrap much as the biggest change was to my blog section and homepage.

I have used my final wireframes based off the feedback and have made an almost fully functioning website. The websites functionality felt clean and straight to the point, just like how I wanted it to be and overall, I am very happy with my progress. I have a few more functions and pages to implement and then its safe to say that I have created my first proper website!

I definitely feel that I have hit all the goals I was aiming for so far. It was thanks to the readings and research I have done that helped me create good UI/UX functions. I still, however, have a lot to work and improve on, Especially when it comes to semantic markup. Ive noticed that whist grouping things, i often create too many containers for items, and because of this my CSS styling page is becoming very long and often confusing. I also get confused when referencing back to my HTML code because of this.

I have a much bigger picture for my website and further designs I want to implement but I am unsure if I will have enough time to do so as redoing some of my pages cost me time.

I will definitely aim to achieve these goals to the best of my ability, nonetheless.
